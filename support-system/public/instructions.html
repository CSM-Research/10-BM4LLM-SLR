BM4LLM-SLR

Experiment Instructions

Systematic Literature Reviews (SLRs) are structured methodologies used to identify, evaluate, and synthesize relevant studies on a particular topic. They are crucial in building scientific evidence. However, conducting an SLR is often labor-intensive, time-consuming, and prone to inconsistencies when done manually.

Recently, many researchers have started using Large Language Models (LLMs) to support tasks in SLRs. Despite their potential, recent evidence suggests that LLMs alone are not yet reliable enough for fully automated literature reviews. Our goal in this experiment is to validate a model that makes LLM-generated outputs more trustworthy and controllable.

BM4LLM-SLR - Bias Mitigation for LLM-Assisted SLR

Our method is structured into two layers. The first layer performs an initial selection based purely on LLMs. In this step, you will configure which LLM you want to use and define the prompt. The second layer adjusts the classification behavior by setting parameters such as desired confidence, strictness, and the number of reviewers involved.
